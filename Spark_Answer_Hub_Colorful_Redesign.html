How Children Learn at Spark Academy — The Purposeful Play Framework

The Purposeful Play Framework is Spark Academy's proprietary, research-driven curriculum framework, developed by Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. and built on the Experience Early Learning whole-child curriculum. It integrates individualized developmental assessment, purposeful play, daily enrichment, and the Developmental Playroom into a cohesive approach to early childhood education.

Purposeful play is the researched method through which young children build neural pathways for language, problem-solving, self-regulation, and social-emotional development. It is neither unstructured free play nor rigid, worksheet-based academics. Every activity at Spark is designed to feel like play to the child while building specific developmental skills identified through individualized assessment.

Five Components of the Purposeful Play Framework

Experience Early Learning Foundation Whole-child curriculum covering academic, social-emotional, physical, and creative development. Not just math and reading — the full spectrum of what young children need.
Individual Assessment System Formal assessment tools identify where each child is developmentally. Teachers build custom learning paths for each student, not for the class as a whole.
Daily Structure A consistent daily rhythm: Academic Play (1.5–2.5 hours) + Developmental Playroom (30 min) + Daily Enrichment (30 min) + Outdoor Play (15 min) + Snack & Bathroom (15 min).
Enrichment Rotation Five daily enrichments built into every program: Spanish (Monday), STEM (Tuesday), Art (Wednesday), Communication (Thursday), Music & Movement (Friday).
Purposeful Play Philosophy Activities are designed to feel like play while building specific developmental skills. Play is the vehicle. Purpose is the engine.

The Developmental Playroom

The Developmental Playroom is a dedicated space, separate from the main classroom, that transforms completely every month to align with curriculum themes. It is the one space at Spark where children lead the play entirely — teachers support, encourage, and ensure safety, but do not direct the activity. Children choose how to explore, what to build, who to collaborate with, and how to solve the problems they encounter. Pre-academic skills are intentionally embedded into every monthly setup.

No other preschool in the Morton or Peoria area offers a comparable dedicated, monthly-rotating play environment.

Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. — Founder and Executive Director

Dr. Michelle Peterson is the founder and executive director of Spark Academy. She holds a Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) in PK–12 Educational Administration from Illinois State University, a Master of Science in Educational Administration from Bradley University, and a Bachelor of Science in Applied Learning and Development from The University of Texas at Austin. She holds four Illinois teaching and administrative certifications, including the Superintendent Certificate, Chief School Business Officer (CSBO) Certificate, and Type 75 Administrative Certificate.

Dr. Peterson's doctoral research at Illinois State University focused on peer engagement strategies for children with autism in inclusive preschool settings, using Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Intervention (NDBI) frameworks. Before founding Spark Academy, she served as assistant principal at Lincoln Elementary in Morton for 10 years, where she led PBIS implementation, school improvement planning, and special education programming. Her career spans more than 20 years in education, from kindergarten classroom teaching in Texas to doctoral-level research and school leadership.

She is the only doctoral-level early childhood education researcher operating a preschool in Central Illinois.
