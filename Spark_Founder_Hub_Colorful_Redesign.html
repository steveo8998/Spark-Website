Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. — Educator, Researcher, and the Founder Who Built Spark for Children Like Yours

The only preschool in Central Illinois founded and led by a doctoral researcher in early childhood education.

Some educators study early childhood development. Others spend decades in the classroom. Dr. Michelle Peterson did both — and then built a school to put it all into practice.

This page is her story — the career that led here, the research that drives the curriculum, and the personal conviction that every child deserves a beginning built on evidence, not tradition.

A Collision of Professional Life and Having Children of Her Own

Dr. Peterson spent the first decade of her career in the classroom — teaching kindergarten in Texas, fourth and fifth grade in Peoria, and then moving into school administration, where she served as an assistant principal for 10 years at Lincoln Elementary in Morton. She led school improvement teams, co-chaired PBIS implementation, coached teachers, and drove curriculum alignment across an entire building.

Then she became a mother.

When her own children started preschool, she experienced the local early childhood landscape from the other side of the table. What she found didn't match what she knew the research said was possible.

Worksheets with copyrights from 1987. Classrooms staffed by well-meaning adults without credentials in early childhood development. Waitlists so long that families who moved to the area after a child's birth had no chance of getting a spot. A gap between what the science of early learning demands and what the options in Central Illinois actually delivered.

So she built the school she was looking for.

She filed her LLC in 2022. After two years of meticulous planning — designing the curriculum, building out the space, assembling a team of degreed and credentialed teachers — Spark Academy opened its doors on July 10, 2023.

The school was designed to feel homelike. Many children walk in and kick off their shoes because it feels like they belong. Every classroom, every enrichment rotation, every corner of the Developmental Play Room was built to serve one purpose: giving young children the kind of beginning that research says they deserve.

She designed a school where parents don't drop their children off at a locked door and hope for the best. At Spark, families walk inside — into the classroom, into their child's day — because Dr. Peterson believes the best schools are the ones that welcome scrutiny, not avoid it.

A Personal Connection to Inclusive Education

Dr. Peterson's dedication to inclusive education is deeply personal — rooted in growing up alongside a family member with autism. That experience shaped her understanding, long before her doctoral research confirmed it, that every child deserves an environment where they can build friendships, develop confidence, and thrive alongside their peers.

It's one of the reasons her dissertation research at Illinois State University focused on peer engagement in preschool settings — specifically, how naturalistic, play-based interventions help young children with autism develop meaningful social connections. And it's why Spark Academy is built as an inclusive and adaptive learning environment, designed to serve the diverse needs of all young children.

Research Into Practice — Every Day, in Every Classroom

Dr. Peterson didn't just study how young children learn. She built a school that applies those findings every day. Here's what that looks like at Spark Academy.

The Purposeful Play Framework

Spark's proprietary curriculum framework, developed by Dr. Peterson and built on the Experience Early Learning curriculum. Integrates individualized developmental assessment, purposeful play, daily enrichment, and the Developmental Play Room into a cohesive approach grounded in research.

Explore the Purposeful Play Framework →

A 5:1 Student-to-Teacher Ratio

The lowest ratio of any preschool in the area. Classes capped at 10–12 students across 4 sections. This isn't a marketing number — it's what makes individualization possible.

Degreed & Credentialed Teachers

Every teacher at Spark holds a degree and credential in education. “A lot of people just have people who like kids. They don't necessarily have a degree or credential.”

The Developmental Play Room

A dedicated, separate play environment that transforms monthly around new themes. The only playroom of its kind in the area — changed out every month, with pre-academic skills intentionally embedded.

Daily Enrichment Rotation

Five daily enrichments built into every program: Spanish (Mon), STEM (Tue), Art (Wed), Communication (Thu), Music & Movement (Fri). Not after-school extras — core programming.

An Inclusive Learning Environment

Built from the ground up as an inclusive, adaptive environment. Outside providers are welcomed into the building to support individual children. Inclusivity is structural, not a footnote.

320+ Families Served

Since opening in July 2023, Spark has served over 320 families across Central Illinois. A cumulative record of trust.
