What Your Tour Looks Like

No script and no sales pitch. Just a real look at how your child's day would unfold at Spark.

Come inside — you're always welcome here
Unlike schools where tours follow a scripted path and parents never return to the classroom, your tour at Spark starts the same way every school day does: you walk right in. No locked doors. No lobby wait. Just an open school where parents are part of the community.
Walk through every classroom and learning space
You'll see the classrooms for your child's age group, the Developmental Playroom that transforms monthly, and the outdoor play areas. If you visit during class hours, you'll see purposeful play in action.
Meet Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D.
Dr. Peterson leads every tour personally. She'll answer your questions about the curriculum, share how the Purposeful Play Framework would work for your child, and explain how teachers handle big emotions, different learning styles, and the transition to school.
Ask anything — no follow-up calls unless you want them
This is your time. Ask about how Spark supports children with different learning styles, what the enrichment schedule looks like, or how mornings work for a child who's never been to school before. There's no script. There's no pressure.

One more thing: the access you get during your tour isn't special. It's normal. Our parents walk into classrooms every single day. You'll never have to wonder what's happening behind a closed door — because at Spark, the door is always open.

Questions About Visiting Spark

How long does a tour take?
Most tours last about 30 minutes. If you have more questions, we're happy to take as much time as you need.
Should I bring my child?
You're welcome to bring your child, but it's not required. Some parents prefer to visit first on their own so they can focus on asking questions — and that's completely fine. If your child does come along, they're welcome to explore the classroom.
What will I see during the tour?
You'll walk through all of our learning spaces, including the classrooms for your child's age group and the Developmental Playroom. If you visit during class hours, you'll see learning in action. Dr. Peterson leads every tour personally, so you'll have a chance to ask questions directly.
Can both parents come?
Absolutely. We encourage it. Choosing a preschool is a big decision, and we want both parents — or any caregivers involved in the decision — to feel confident and informed.
What if I'm not ready to enroll after the tour?
No pressure at all. Some families tour months before they're ready to enroll. Some visit more than once. We're here whenever you're ready — and if questions come up later, you can always reach out.
